Corona Virus Lab-Confirmed Cases

How are cases identified?

COVID-19 cases are identified by taking specimens from people and sending these specimens to laboratories around the UK to be tested. If the test is positive, this is referred to as a lab-confirmed case.

Note that cases in people who have not been tested are not included in the confirmed case counts.
